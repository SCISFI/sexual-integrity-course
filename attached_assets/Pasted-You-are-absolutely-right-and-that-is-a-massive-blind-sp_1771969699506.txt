### UI/UX Design Brief: Week 1 Crisis Hook ("The Catalyst")

**Context for Designer:** This screen is the critical hook for Week 1 of "The Integrity Protocol". The target audience is men dealing with problematic sexual behavior who have just experienced a major relationship crisis (discovery, betrayal, ultimatum) involving their spouse or partner.

**The Goal:** We need a visually arresting UI component (a hero section or a standalone card) that immediately follows the opening. It must visually represent the weight of the relationship crisis without using cheesy stock photos of fighting couples or people crying. It needs to feel sobering, private, and deeply resonant.

**Vibe & Tone:** High-stakes, secure, empathetic but unflinching. Use our established dark mode or muted "slate" palette.

**Design Requirements:**

* **Layout:** A distinct, elevated card or a stark, full-width section with heavy padding/white space to force the user to slow down and read it.
* **Typography:** The headline should be bold and commanding. The subtext should use a highly readable, slightly muted font to lower the cognitive load.
* **Iconography/Visuals:** Do NOT use stock photography. Instead, use a minimalist, conceptual line-art graphic.
* *Ideas:* Two subtle, divergent lines; a minimalist representation of a ring; a fractured circle; or simply a stark, empty chair. It should symbolize connection under severe strain.


* **Interactive Element:** A slow-fade or delayed reveal for the text could work well here to build tension, but keep it subtle.

**The Copy to Insert into the Design:**

> **Headline:** The Crisis Point.
> 
> 
> **Body:** If you are here, it’s probably because the hiding stopped working. The browser history, the promises, the isolation—it finally collided with the person closest to you.
> 
> 
> You aren't just here to fix a habit. You are here because of the look in her eyes when the truth broke through.
> **Button/Call to Action:** "Acknowledge the Catalyst" (This button transitions them to the first reflection).
