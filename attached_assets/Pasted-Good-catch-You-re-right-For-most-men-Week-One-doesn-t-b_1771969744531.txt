## 📌 Prompt for App Designer

We need to revise Week 1 to explicitly anchor the content in the partner/spouse crisis moment that brought most users here.

This is not optional. It is central to engagement and retention.

### Objective

Position Week 1 as a response to relational rupture — not just compulsive behavior.

Most users arrive after:

* Being discovered
* Confessing
* Being confronted
* Being threatened with separation
* Experiencing emotional distance from their partner

Week 1 must name this clearly.

---

## 🔹 Required Structural Changes

### 1. Insert a New Opening Section (Above All Clinical Content)

Title:

**“If You’re Here Because Something Broke…”**

Content should:

* Directly acknowledge discovery or confrontation
* Validate the emotional shock of a partner’s reaction
* Normalize panic, fear, and relational instability
* Avoid blaming the partner
* Avoid shaming the user
* Emphasize that crisis can become clarity

Tone:
Direct. Calm. Grounded. Not dramatic.

Example anchor language:

* “If your partner discovered something…”
* “If trust was shaken…”
* “If you’re here because someone you love is hurting…”

Close the section with:

> “This program is not just about stopping behavior.
> It is about rebuilding integrity in relationship.”

---

### 2. Add a Relational Reflection Block

Insert immediately after the addiction cycle section.

Title:

**“Relational Impact”**

Prompt users to reflect on:

* How has this affected my partner?
* What has this done to trust?
* What has this done to emotional safety?
* What do I imagine my partner is feeling right now?

Important:
Do not make this accusatory.
Make it awareness-based.

---

### 3. Add a Partner-Focused Exercise

Title:

**“Rebuilding Begins With Understanding”**

Short exercise:

* What does my partner need most right now?
* What am I afraid they will never feel again?
* What would rebuilding trust require from me?

This should not turn into apology scripting.
It should promote ownership and emotional awareness.

---

### 4. Tie Crisis to the Loneliness Framework

Add a bridging paragraph:

Most men interpret discovery as the problem.

But the real problem began long before discovery — in isolation.

Position crisis as:

Isolation exposed.

---

### 5. Update Week 1 Commitment Statement

Replace generic commitment with:

> “I am willing to confront my patterns and rebuild integrity in my relationships.”

The checkbox remains sacred.
No automation changes.

---

## 🔹 UX Requirements

* This crisis section must appear before diagnostic language.
* Avoid overwhelming with text.
* Use short paragraphs and white space.
* Avoid clinical jargon in the opening section.
* Keep tone steady and regulated.

---

## 🔹 Clinical Guardrails

* Do not assign blame to partner.
* Do not promise relationship repair.
* Do not imply reconciliation is guaranteed.
* Keep responsibility with the user.

---

## End Goal

Week 1 should feel like:

Recognition
Ownership
Relational awareness
Hope grounded in responsibility

Not:

Education
Diagnosis
Information

---

If you implement this correctly, the user should feel:

“I am here because something broke.
This program understands that.
And it knows what to do next.”
