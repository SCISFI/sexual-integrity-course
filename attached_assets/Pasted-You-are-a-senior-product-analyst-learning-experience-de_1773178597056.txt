I am evaluating Week 1 of my app, Integrity Protocol, and I need a rigorous product improvement report based on real user data.

Context:
- Audience: men who struggle with problematic sexual behavior
- Week 1 title: "The Moment You Stop Pretending"
- Week 1 goal: help users practice honesty, name what brought them here, understand the behavioral cycle that has been running them, and decide whether they are willing to do something different
- My goal: determine how successful Week 1 is, how well the app is drawing out meaningful user insight, where the experience is working, where it is failing, and what to improve immediately

I will provide:
- Week 1 lesson content and instructional copy if available
- Reflection prompts
- Exercise prompts
- User responses
- Completion/progress data
- Any related mentor or therapist feedback
- Any metadata on skipped answers, timestamps, drop-off, or partial completion

Your job:
Analyze all Week 1 user data across users and produce a critical, evidence-based product improvement report.

Do not flatter the product.
Do not assume the content is effective.
Do not merely summarize.
Diagnose what is actually happening.

Evaluate the data in these categories:

1. USER RESPONSE QUALITY
Assess the quality of reflection and exercise responses across users:
- depth vs superficiality
- specificity vs vagueness
- honesty vs performative answering
- signs of confusion, avoidance, minimization, defensiveness, or disengagement
- whether users actually understood the core Week 1 concepts
- whether responses show genuine self-confrontation or just task completion

2. CROSS-USER PATTERNS
Compare users and identify:
- recurring emotional themes
- repeated misunderstandings
- recurring weak-response patterns
- prompts that consistently generate useful insight
- prompts that consistently generate shallow or low-signal answers
- where users appear most engaged
- where users lose momentum, clarity, or willingness

3. CONTENT EFFECTIVENESS
Evaluate whether the Week 1 educational content is doing its job:
- Is the framing clear?
- Is the sequence effective?
- Is the content too abstract, too moralizing, too obvious, too dense, or too leading?
- Does it create psychological safety for honesty?
- Does it help users identify their behavioral cycle?
- Does it invite insight, or just compliance?
- Are users being asked the right questions at the right time?

4. INTERACTION AND UX QUALITY
Evaluate the app experience itself:
- friction points
- prompt fatigue
- repetitive or poorly sequenced interactions
- places where the interface may encourage low-effort answers
- whether users likely needed examples, scaffolding, or adaptive follow-up prompts
- whether the experience feels static when it should feel responsive
- missed opportunities for branching, follow-up, or deeper inquiry

5. VULNERABILITIES AND MISSED MARKS
Identify vulnerabilities in the product and learning design:
- ambiguous wording
- prompts that invite socially desirable answers
- insufficient emotional containment or safety
- weak onboarding into reflection
- assumptions users may not share
- places where shame, resistance, or defensiveness may block insight
- places where the app collects text but fails to generate meaningful self-awareness
- any safety, trust, retention, or interpretation risks visible in the current design

6. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
For each major weakness, identify the most likely root cause:
- weak prompt design
- weak lesson framing
- poor sequence
- lack of context
- poor emotional timing
- insufficient examples
- cognitive overload
- lack of adaptive follow-up
- interface constraints
- motivation mismatch
- other structural issues

7. RECOMMENDATIONS
Give practical recommendations in priority order:
A. Immediate fixes
B. Prompt rewrites
C. Content improvements
D. UX/app flow improvements
E. Better data collection
F. Experiments to run before or during Week 2
